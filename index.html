The Research

The Science of Happiness 🧠

🧬

The 50/10/40 Rule

Harvard research shows 50% of happiness is genetic, 10% is circumstances — but 40% is fully in your control through daily choices.

Psychology
🤝

Relationships #1

The Harvard Study of Adult Development (80 years!) found that the quality of our relationships is the single biggest predictor of happiness.

Social Science
🙏

Gratitude Rewires You

Writing 3 things you're grateful for daily increases dopamine and serotonin — the same effect as antidepressants, naturally.

Neuroscience
🏃

Movement = Mood

Just 20 minutes of exercise releases endorphins that reduce stress and anxiety and boosts mood for up to 12 hours.

Biology
🎯

Flow State

Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi found that happiness peaks when we're fully absorbed in a meaningful challenge — called "flow."

Positive Psychology
💸

Money & Happiness

Studies show happiness increases with income up to ~₹6 LPA for daily wellbeing — beyond that, experiences and meaning matter more.

Economics
Daily Practice

10 Science-Backed Happiness Habits 🌱

1

Morning Gratitude

Write 3 things you're grateful for every morning to rewire your brain for positivity.

2

Move Your Body

Even a 20-minute walk boosts mood significantly. Exercise is nature's antidepressant.

3

Mindful Breathing

5 minutes of deep breathing activates your parasympathetic nervous system and reduces cortisol.

4

Acts of Kindness

Doing something kind for others boosts your own happiness more than doing something for yourself.

5

Digital Sunset

Avoid screens 1 hour before bed. Quality sleep is the foundation of emotional wellbeing.

6

Connect Deeply

Have one real, meaningful conversation daily. Surface-level chats don't count!

7

Learn Something New

Curiosity and learning triggers dopamine. It keeps the brain young and joyful.

8

Spend Time in Nature

Just 20 minutes outdoors reduces stress hormones and elevates mood measurably.

9

Practice Forgiveness

Holding grudges hurts you more than them. Forgiveness is a gift you give yourself.

10

Celebrate Small Wins

Don't wait for big achievements. Acknowledge every small step — your brain loves it.
